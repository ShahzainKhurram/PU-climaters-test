About Climate Change

Climate change refers to significant changes in global temperatures and weather patterns over time. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, recent trends are largely driven by human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, which increases greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere.

Learn More

Causes of Climate Change

Industrial Emissions

The release of greenhouse gases from factories and power plants contributes significantly to global warming.

Deforestation

Cutting down forests reduces the Earth's capacity to absorb carbon dioxide, exacerbating climate change.

Transportation

Vehicles powered by fossil fuels emit significant amounts of carbon dioxide and other pollutants.

Climate Mitigation Strategies

Direct Air Capture (DAC)

DAC technology captures CO2 directly from the atmosphere. It has a global impact but is currently expensive and energy-intensive.

Enhanced Weathering

This method involves applying crushed silicate minerals to accelerate CO2 sequestration in soil and agricultural areas.

Biochar

Biochar, produced by heating organic material in the absence of oxygen, improves soil fertility and sequesters carbon but can be costly to produce.

Seed Balls

These small balls of seeds, clay, and compost are used in reforestation, though they may be less effective in degraded soils.

Effects of Climate Change

Droughts

Longer and more intense droughts affect crops, livestock, and water supplies, leading to food and water shortages.

Rising Sea Levels

Melting ice caps and glaciers lead to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities with flooding and habitat loss.
