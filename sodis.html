Solar Disinfection (SODIS)

Solar Disinfection (SODIS) is a simple, low-cost method to disinfect water using sunlight.

How SODIS Works:

  1. Fill a transparent plastic bottle with contaminated water.
  2. Leave the bottle in direct sunlight for at least 6 hours.
  3. UV-A radiation from sunlight kills bacteria, viruses, and parasites.

Benefits:

  • Low-cost: No special equipment or chemicals needed.
  • Easy to use: Simple, user-friendly method.
  • Effective: Removes 99.9% of bacteria, viruses, and parasites.
  • Sustainable: Uses renewable solar energy.

Guidelines:

  1. Bottle material: Transparent PET (polyethylene terephthalate) or glass.
  2. Bottle size: 1-2 liters.
  3. Water volume: Fill bottle to 80-90% capacity.
  4. Sunlight exposure: 6-8 hours in direct sunlight.
  5. Weather conditions: Clear skies, no shading.

Effectiveness:

  • Bacteria: 99.9% removal (E. coli, Salmonella, etc.)
  • Viruses: 99.9% removal (Rotavirus, Norovirus, etc.)
  • Parasites: 99.9% removal (Giardia, Cryptosporidium, etc.)

Limitations:

  • Turbidity: Water with high turbidity may require additional treatment.
  • Organic pollutants: SODIS may not remove chemical contaminants.
  • Overcast conditions: Reduced effectiveness in cloudy weather.

Implementation:

  • Household level: Individual families can use SODIS.
  • Community level: SODIS can be integrated into community water treatment systems.
  • Emergency response: SODIS can provide safe drinking water during disasters.

Case Studies:

  • Kenya: SODIS implemented in rural communities, reducing waterborne illnesses by 50%.
  • India: SODIS integrated into school water treatment systems, improving student health.
